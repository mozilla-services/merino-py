# Merino AI Policy

This policy extends the
[Firefox AI Coding Policy](https://firefox-source-docs.mozilla.org/contributing/ai-coding.html)
with guidelines specific to the Merino project. The Firefox policy applies in full; what follows adds to it.

## Philosophy

Merino is a privacy-preserving web service maintained by humans. We center the codebase around humans: no matter the source of help we get from the outside, humans read, deploy, and operate this code. Humans are accountable for outages and responsible for the craft behind novel solutions. As long as this is true, we optimize for human understanding.

AI tools can help you explore the codebase, bounce ideas, review your own
code before submitting, or produce solutions when the problem is small and well-defined.
These are all good uses. Thus, responsible and policy-guided AI usage is welcome for Merino.

However, AI models are trained on pre-existing codebases. They are good at recombining what already exists. Novel solutions, the kind that come from deeply understanding a problem and its constraints, still require the craft and judgment of a human who has done the work to master it. Use AI as a tool to elevate your skills, not to replace them.

## Responsibilities

We respect and enforce the [Firefox AI Coding Policy responsibilities](https://firefox-source-docs.mozilla.org/contributing/ai-coding.html#responsibilities).
